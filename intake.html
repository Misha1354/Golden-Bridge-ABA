🔒 1. Notice of Privacy Practices (HIPAA) *

Golden Bridge ABA is required by law to maintain the privacy and security of your protected health information (PHI) and to provide you with this Notice of Privacy Practices. We may use and disclose your PHI for treatment, payment, and healthcare operations without requiring your separate authorization.

How We Use and Disclose Your PHI:

  • Treatment: We may use and share PHI to coordinate care with other providers (therapists, physicians, schools) involved in your child’s treatment.
  • Payment: We may use and share PHI to bill and collect payment from your insurance company or other payers.
  • Healthcare Operations: We may use PHI for quality improvement, staff training, compliance reviews, and business administration.
  • Appointment Reminders: We may contact you with appointment reminders via phone, text, or email.
  • Required by Law: We will disclose PHI as required by federal, state, or local law (e.g., mandatory reporting of abuse or neglect).

Your Rights Regarding Your PHI:

  • Right to Access: You may request a copy of your child’s health record.
  • Right to Amend: You may request corrections to your child’s health record.
  • Right to an Accounting of Disclosures: You may request a list of certain disclosures we have made of your PHI.
  • Right to Restrict: You may request restrictions on how we use or disclose PHI (we are not required to agree in all cases).
  • Right to Confidential Communications: You may request that we communicate with you in a specific way or at a specific location.
  • Right to a Paper Copy of This Notice: You may request a paper copy of this Notice at any time.

How to File a Complaint: If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with us or with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services Office for Civil Rights at www.hhs.gov/ocr/privacy. You will not be retaliated against for filing a complaint.

Contact: Golden Bridge ABA Privacy Officer — michael@goldaba.com

💌 2. Consent to ABA Treatment *

I consent to evaluation and Applied Behavior Analysis therapy services for my child/dependent at Golden Bridge ABA. I understand that ABA is an evidence-based behavioral intervention, and that my active participation as a caregiver is an important component of my child’s treatment.

I authorize Golden Bridge ABA to conduct an initial assessment and provide ongoing Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) therapy services for my child or dependent. I understand the following:

  • ABA therapy is a behavioral intervention approach based on the science of learning and behavior. It is widely recognized as an evidence-based treatment for Autism Spectrum Disorder and other developmental conditions.
  • Treatment will be individualized and based on a comprehensive assessment. A BCBA (Board Certified Behavior Analyst) will design and supervise the treatment program.
  • The treatment plan will include measurable goals, data collection, and regular review. The plan may be modified based on my child’s progress and response to treatment.
  • Caregiver participation, including parent/guardian training sessions, is expected and is a core part of the treatment model. Participation enhances the effectiveness and generalization of skills.
  • I may withdraw consent for any specific procedure or for continued treatment at any time by notifying Golden Bridge ABA in writing.
  • ABA therapy does not guarantee specific outcomes. Progress varies by individual. The clinical team will communicate regularly about my child’s progress.

Voluntary: I understand that I am authorizing this voluntarily and that my child’s treatment will not be conditioned on my signing this authorization, except where disclosure is necessary to obtain payment for services.

Notice of Privacy Practices

Effective Date: January 1, 2025  |  This notice describes how protected health information about you may be used and disclosed and how you can get access to this information. Please review it carefully.

Our Legal Duty

Golden Bridge ABA (“we,” “us,” “our”) is required by law to maintain the privacy and security of your protected health information (PHI). We are required to abide by the terms of this Notice and to provide you with notice of our privacy practices. We reserve the right to change the terms of this Notice and to make the new Notice provisions effective for all PHI we maintain. We will post a current copy of this Notice on our website and provide one upon request.

How We May Use and Disclose Your PHI

Uses and Disclosures That Do Not Require Your Authorization

We may use and disclose PHI without your written authorization for the following purposes:

  • Treatment: We may use or disclose PHI for treatment purposes, including sharing information with other healthcare providers involved in your child’s care (e.g., physicians, specialists, schools, therapists) to coordinate and manage treatment.
  • Payment: We may use or disclose PHI to obtain payment for services, including submitting claims to insurance companies, verifying coverage, obtaining prior authorizations, and collecting payment.
  • Healthcare Operations: We may use or disclose PHI for our healthcare operations, including quality improvement activities, staff training and supervision, compliance activities, accreditation, and business management.
  • Appointment Reminders: We may contact you with appointment reminders using the contact information you have provided (phone, text, email). You may request alternative means of contact.
  • Required by Law: We will disclose PHI when required to do so by federal, state, or local law. This includes mandatory reporting of suspected child abuse or neglect, responses to court orders or subpoenas, and public health reporting.
  • Public Health Activities: We may disclose PHI to public health authorities for activities such as preventing or controlling disease or injury.
  • Health Oversight Activities: We may disclose PHI to health oversight agencies for activities authorized by law, such as audits and investigations.
  • Serious Threats to Health or Safety: We may use or disclose PHI if we believe it is necessary to prevent or lessen a serious and imminent threat to the health or safety of a person or the public.

Uses and Disclosures That Require Your Written Authorization

For most uses and disclosures beyond treatment, payment, and healthcare operations — including the use of PHI for marketing purposes, sale of PHI, and psychotherapy notes — we will obtain your written authorization. You may revoke any authorization you provide at any time by submitting a written request to Golden Bridge ABA.

Your Rights Regarding Your Protected Health Information

Right to Access and Inspect

You have the right to inspect and obtain a copy of your child’s PHI in our records, including medical and billing records, for as long as we maintain them. We may charge a reasonable cost-based fee for providing copies. We may deny access in limited circumstances as allowed by law.

Right to Amend

If you believe that PHI we maintain about your child is incorrect or incomplete, you may request that we amend the information. We may deny the request if we determine the information is accurate and complete, was not created by us, or if we do not have the information. If we deny the request, we will explain why in writing.

Right to an Accounting of Disclosures

You have the right to request a list of certain disclosures we have made of your child’s PHI in the prior six years. This accounting does not include disclosures made for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations, or disclosures you authorized.

Right to Request Restrictions

You have the right to request restrictions on how we use or disclose PHI for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations, or to persons involved in your care. We are not required to agree to all restrictions. However, if we agree to a restriction, we will honor it except in emergencies. We are required to honor restrictions on disclosures to health plans if you pay for a service in full out-of-pocket and request the restriction.

Right to Confidential Communications

You have the right to request that we communicate with you about PHI by alternative means or at an alternative location (e.g., contacting you only by cell phone or only at a specific address). We will accommodate reasonable requests.

Right to a Paper Copy of This Notice

You have the right to receive a paper copy of this Notice at any time, even if you have received it electronically. To obtain a paper copy, contact us at: michael@goldaba.com.

Our Duties

Golden Bridge ABA is required by law to: maintain the privacy of PHI; provide individuals with notice of our legal duties and privacy practices with respect to PHI; notify affected individuals following a breach of unsecured PHI; abide by the terms of the Notice currently in effect; and not use or disclose PHI in any way not described in this Notice without your written authorization.

Changes to This Notice

We reserve the right to change this Notice and to make the revised Notice effective for PHI we already have, as well as any new information received in the future. We will post a copy of the current Notice at our office and on our website.

How to File a Complaint

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with Golden Bridge ABA or with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) Office for Civil Rights. You will not be penalized or retaliated against in any way for filing a complaint.
