Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA)

Massachusetts New Mexico In-Home & In-School
$80 – $100 per hour

About the Role

As a BCBA at Golden Bridge ABA, you will design, oversee, and continuously refine individualized ABA programs for children on the autism spectrum and with related developmental disabilities. You will work closely with families, schools, and RBTs to deliver high-quality, evidence-based care in natural environments.

Responsibilities

  • Conduct functional behavior assessments (FBAs) and skills assessments
  • Develop individualized treatment plans and behavior intervention plans (BIPs)
  • Provide ongoing supervision and training to RBTs
  • Collaborate with parents, caregivers, and school teams
  • Monitor client progress through data analysis and adjust programming accordingly
  • Participate in IEP meetings and team consultations as needed
  • Maintain accurate clinical documentation

Requirements

  • Current BCBA certification in good standing
  • Active state licensure (MA or NM as applicable)
  • Experience working with children with ASD or related diagnoses
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills
  • Reliable transportation and ability to travel to client homes and schools
  • Cleared background check

Registered Behavior Technician (RBT)

Massachusetts New Mexico In-Home & In-School
$22 – $30 per hour

About the Role

As an RBT at Golden Bridge ABA, you will directly implement ABA therapy programs under the close supervision of a BCBA. You'll work one-on-one with children in their homes and schools, building meaningful skills through play-based, naturalistic teaching. No two days are the same — and the impact you make is real.

Responsibilities

  • Implement individualized ABA programs as designed by the supervising BCBA
  • Collect accurate behavioral and skill acquisition data every session
  • Build strong, trusting relationships with clients and families
  • Communicate session updates clearly to the supervising BCBA
  • Follow behavior intervention plans consistently and with fidelity
  • Participate in regular supervision and team meetings

Requirements

  • Current RBT certification (or willingness to obtain before start date)
  • High school diploma or equivalent required; bachelor's degree preferred
  • Experience working with children preferred
  • Compassionate, patient, and energetic approach
  • Reliable transportation and ability to travel to client locations
  • Cleared background check
