What to Expect

What Is Home-Based ABA Therapy?

Home-based ABA therapy is one-on-one Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) delivered in your child's home — where learning happens inside the real moments of daily life: mealtimes, transitions, sibling play, getting dressed, settling down for sleep. A Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA) designs an individualized program, and a trained Registered Behavior Technician (RBT) runs daily sessions, with regular BCBA supervision and parent coaching built in.

One-on-One in Your Westford Home

Sessions take place in the spaces your child knows best. Skills built at the kitchen table or in the playroom carry over faster than skills built in a clinic.

BCBA-Designed, RBT-Delivered

A Board Certified Behavior Analyst writes the plan, sets the goals, and supervises every case. A trained RBT runs your child's home-based sessions on a consistent schedule.

Built Around Your Family

Goals reflect what actually matters to your family — communicating wants, handling transitions, joining siblings in play, building independence with daily routines.

FAQ

Home-Based ABA Therapy in Westford — Common Questions

What is home-based ABA therapy?

Home-based ABA therapy is one-on-one Applied Behavior Analysis delivered in your child's home, where learning is woven into real routines — mealtimes, transitions, play, sleep. A BCBA designs the program, and a trained RBT runs daily sessions in your Westford home, with regular BCBA oversight and parent training.

Do you provide home-based ABA therapy services in Westford, MA?

Yes. Golden Bridge ABA provides home-based ABA therapy services across Westford, including Westford Center, Graniteville, Forge Village, and Nabnasset, plus in-school support with Westford Public Schools.

Do you work with Westford Public Schools?

Yes. Our BCBAs and RBTs collaborate with Westford Public Schools — including Robinson, Miller, Day, Crisafulli, Stony Brook, Blanchard, and Westford Academy — and with private schools across the Nashoba Valley region. Goals are aligned to each student's IEP.

Is home-based ABA therapy in Westford covered by MassHealth?

Yes. MassHealth covers home-based ABA therapy for children with autism throughout Westford. We're also in-network with Blue Cross Blue Shield of MA, Tufts Health Plan, Aetna, and Cigna. We verify your benefits at no cost.

How quickly can my child start home-based ABA therapy in Westford?

Most Westford families complete intake and insurance verification within 1–3 weeks. Once approved, we match your child with a Westford-area RBT supervised by a BCBA and begin home sessions on your schedule.

How many hours per week of home-based ABA therapy will my child receive?

Hours are individualized based on your child's assessment, age, and goals — typically anywhere from 10 to 30+ hours per week. Your BCBA recommends a starting schedule and adjusts as your child progresses.
